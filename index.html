Questions you definitely have

Does it actually measure cortisol?

Nope. Real cortisol measurement requires a blood or saliva test. This measures your heart rate variability (HRV), which is a legit stress indicator used in clinical research. When you're stressed, your HRV drops and the needle goes red. Is it medically accurate? No. Is it fun, kinda right, and an incredible conversation piece? Absolutely.

Is the needle actually mechanical?

Yes. It uses the same type of stepper motor found in car dashboards (the ones that move your speedometer needle). It physically clicks and sweeps across the gauge face. It's not a screen. It's not an LED. It's a real, moving needle.

How long does the battery last?

1-3 days depending on settings. It takes a reading every 30 seconds and sleeps between measurements. Charges via USB-C — same cable as your phone.

Is it waterproof?

No. It's splash-resistant (wash your hands, you're fine) but don't swim or shower with it. The analog gauge mechanism doesn't love water.

Do I need a phone app?

Nope. Zero setup. Put it on and it works. No Bluetooth, no Wi-Fi, no account, no subscription. We might add an optional companion app later, but the bracelet works completely standalone.
